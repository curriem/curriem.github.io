Research

About me: I am a first-year graduate student at the University of Washington pursuing a dual-title PhD in Astronomy and Astrobiology. I am a member of UW's Virtual Planetary Laboratory and conduct research under the advisement of Prof. Vikki Meadows. Broadly, I work to understand the atmospheres of exoplanets and the properties that make an atmosphere habitable. I am interested in searching for biosignatures, which involves combing through false positive scenarios for one or more gases in an atmosphere. This is achieved through running numerical models to simulate how photochemistry and the climate of a planet affect the atmosphere. A radiative transfer calculation is subsequently applied to these models to begin investigating the detectability of the various molecule species in the atmosphere. The spectra are calculated in high-resolution to prepare for the observations that will be made with the next generation of large ground-based observatories.
